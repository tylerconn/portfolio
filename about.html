About Me

I grew up in South Carolina, where I spent most of my life through college. Originally I majored in Business Administration, but in my Sophomore year, I switched my major to Accounting because I discovered that it was something that I was good at in an introductory course. I enjoyed how it felt like a puzzle to solve and, as a generally curious person, getting to sort through the numbers to find the answer was interesting to me. At this time, I also added a minor in Spanish because I wanted to study the language abroad in Spain.

Following graduation, I took a job as auditor with Ernst & Young in Atlanta, where I worked for 3 years. After that I decided that just being an auditor wasn't for me and I wanted to be a part of growing a company rather than just auditing them, so I took a revenue accounting position with Equifax where I have been for 3 years. During this time I began realizing that being able to express some creativity in my work was important to me and I wasn’t getting that opportunity as an accountant. I always joke that the really “creative” accountants end up in jail. So I began thinking what my next move would be. Then I learned more about ux design

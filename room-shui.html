Room Shui: A Room Design Solution

My Role
UX Designer
My Team

Marissa Patterson (UX Researcher)

Leslie Wong (UX Researcher, Designer)

Lynn Faiola (Front-End Developer)

Tools
Miro, Figma, Whimsical, VS Code, Github
Timeframe
3 weeks
Deliverables
High Fidelity Prototype & Website

The goal of this project was to evaluate an existing government website, identify any problems with the user experience, and redesign the website to address these problems.

Who's the User?

Once had settled on the idea of designing and developing a website to help with room organization/design, our team set about trying to understand who would use something like this and what their specific needs would be. We conducted five user interviews and distributed an online survey that received 37 responses. From this research, we learned that the majority of those surveyed rearrange a room at least once a year, with another quarter of respondents rearranging every few years. We also learned that many of our respondents would like to design their spaces better and more frequently, but they don't know where to start and the process seems overwhelming.

With these insights we created a persona for our user, Edward, who is a real estate agent that spends a lot of time coming up with ideas for how to stage his homes. While we do not anticipate our user base will consist of only realtors, we chose to make our user persona for this project a realtor, because we believed they would be more frequent users and they needs would be more extensive.

In addition to the user persona, we created an empathy map to help further visualize who our user is and find ways we can help.

Next, we developed a storyboard to explore the user's experience and how we would like our product to fit into that experience.

What's the Problem?

With these insights and understanding of the user in mind we drafted a problem statement and hypothesis.

Problem Statement

Individuals between the ages of 25 and 40 do not often redesign an entire room because it's difficult to mentally visualize a better layout. Additionally, experimenting with new ideas takes a great deal of effort as it usually involves moving furniture around in a crowded space. As a result, people often just accumulate objects over time and place them where they fit. The resulting layout does not always meet their needs or make them happy.

Hypothesis

We believe that creating a website that allows people to design their spaces virtually using renderings of real-world objects and spaces will make it easier for them to create spaces that meet their needs.

What's the Solution?

Now we began brainstorming exactly how we would execute this hypothesis and solve the user’s problem. Together, we created a “I like, I wish, What if” analysis to show what we know the user likes, what they wish they had that could provide immediate improvements to the user, and some possibilities that could be implimented in future iterations that may also provide value to our user.

Once we had some ideas for what we wanted to include in our product, our team put together a user flow outlining the steps the user would take navigating our product and we began drafting sketches of how each page would look.

After we discussed our sketches and made a decision on the basic layout of the site, I began designing a low-fidelity prototype using Figma.
